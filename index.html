Why it matters

The real game isn't on the screen

Something clicks the first time a kid sees someone else playing a game they made.

They go from "I played a game" to "I made a game." That's not a small shift. That's the moment they realize they can build things — not just consume them.

We're not teaching kids to code. We're giving them a place where building is the game.

"Every kid has a game inside them. They just need a place to build it."
